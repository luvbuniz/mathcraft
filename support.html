Frequently asked

Do I need to download anything?

No. Stackadoo runs right in your browser. On a tablet or phone you can “Add to Home Screen” for an app-like icon that even works offline.

Is it really safe for kids?

Yes. There’s no advertising, no chat, no contact with strangers, and no purchasable in-game currency. The only purchase is one optional unlock, made by a parent.

What ages is it for?

It’s tuned for grades 2–5 (roughly ages 7–11), with difficulty that adapts up and down to fit your child.

I paid but a world is still locked.

The unlock saves on the device you bought it on. Make sure you returned to the game after paying. Still stuck? Email support@stackadoo.com and we’ll sort it out fast.

How do I get a refund?

Just ask within 30 days — see the refund policy below. No hoops.

Privacy Policy

Your child’s privacy comes first

The short version: we don’t collect personal information from children, and we never sell data. Period.

What we collect

Essentially nothing. Stackadoo has no accounts, no logins, and no sign-up. We do not ask for a child’s name, email, age, location, photo, or any other personal detail. The optional nickname and avatar a child picks are stored only on your own device.

Where game progress lives

Your child’s saves (houses, blocks, badges, settings) are stored locally on your device using your browser’s storage. They never leave the device and are never sent to us. Clearing your browser data removes them.

No tracking, no ads

We don’t use advertising, advertising networks, behavioral tracking, or third-party analytics that profile children. There are no third-party ad or social trackers embedded in the game.

Payments

If you choose the optional one-time unlock, payment is handled entirely by Stripe, a trusted payment processor. We never see or store your full card number. Stripe processes your payment details under its own privacy policy. We only learn that a purchase succeeded.

Children’s privacy (COPPA)

Stackadoo is designed to comply with the U.S. Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). Because we do not collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13, there is nothing for us to share, sell, or disclose. If you believe we have inadvertently received any personal information from a child, email us and we will delete it promptly.

Changes

If this policy changes, we’ll update the date at the top of this page. Questions? support@stackadoo.com.
