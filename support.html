Frequently asked

Do I need to download anything?

No. Stackadoo runs right in your browser. On a tablet or phone you can “Add to Home Screen” for an app-like icon that even works offline.

Is it really safe for kids?

Yes. There’s no advertising, no chat, no contact with strangers, and no purchasable in-game currency. The only purchase is one optional unlock, made by a parent.

What ages is it for?

It’s tuned for grades 2–5 (roughly ages 7–11), with difficulty that adapts up and down to fit your child.

What do I get when I unlock?

Math Meadow is free forever. The one-time unlock makes you a Founding Family: you get the early/beta version other parents don’t have yet, the paid worlds (Reading Town today), and every world and update we add later — free, for life. One payment, never again, no subscription.

Can my child accidentally delete their game?

We protect against it two ways. Deleting a saved game always requires typing a randomly shown code, and the 🗑️ delete buttons are hidden by default for Grades 1–3. You can change this any time in the in-game Grown-ups page (the “Game deletes” setting): hide the buttons for everyone, or show them for older kids—they’ll still need the code.

I paid but a world is still locked.

The unlock saves on the device you bought it on — make sure you returned to the game after paying. Still stuck? Email support@stackadoo.com and we’ll get you unlocked right away.

Can my kids play on more than one device?

Yes. The unlock saves on the device you bought it on; to use it on another tablet or phone, email support@stackadoo.com and we’ll send a restore code to type into the game’s “Have a code?” box. Your purchase is good for your family for life.

How do I get a refund?

Just ask within 30 days — see the refund policy below. No hoops.

Privacy Policy

Your child’s privacy comes first

The short version: we don’t collect personal information from children, and we never sell data. Period.

What we collect

Almost nothing — and never from a child. We do not ask for a child’s name, email, age, location, photo, or any other personal detail. A child’s nickname and avatar are stored only on your own device. The one optional exception is for grown-ups: if a parent chooses to create an account (see below), we collect that parent’s email address — never a child’s.

Where game progress lives

Your child’s saves (houses, blocks, badges, settings) are stored locally on your device using your browser’s storage. If you don’t sign in, they never leave the device and are never sent to us. Clearing your browser data removes them.

Optional grown-up account & cloud sync

Signing in is optional and meant for a parent. If you choose to create an account (with Google, or an email and password), we store your email address and a copy of your family’s game saves, so your purchase and worlds can follow you to another device. Children are only ever nicknames under your account — we never collect a child’s email or personal details. Sign-in is handled by Google Firebase Authentication. You can sign out any time, and you can ask us to delete your account and its data at support@stackadoo.com. If you never sign in, nothing leaves your device.

No tracking, no ads

We don’t use advertising, advertising networks, behavioral tracking, or third-party analytics that profile children. There are no third-party ad or social trackers embedded in the game.

Payments

If you choose the optional one-time unlock, payment is handled entirely by Stripe, a trusted payment processor. We never see or store your full card number. Stripe processes your payment details under its own privacy policy. We only learn that a purchase succeeded.

Children’s privacy (COPPA)

Stackadoo is designed to comply with the U.S. Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). We do not collect personal information from children. The only personal information we ever collect is an optional parent’s email — and only if a grown-up chooses to create an account — never from a child. We don’t sell or share data. If you believe we have inadvertently received any personal information from a child, email us and we will delete it promptly.

Changes

If this policy changes, we’ll update the date at the top of this page. Questions? support@stackadoo.com.
