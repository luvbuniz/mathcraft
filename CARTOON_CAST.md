# 🎬 Stackadoo World — Cartoon Cast Bible

A reference for the edutainment shorts (YouTube Shorts / TikTok / Reels). Every
character here is an avatar that **already exists in the game**, so the cartoon
cast doubles as in-game characters — kids can *be* them, and later they become
the **Stackadoo teacher NPCs**. Hand this to Grok/Gemini when generating clips.

> Voxel cartoon style, bright and cartoony. Original Stackadoo characters —
> never Minecraft Steve / Creepers / trademarked skins.

---

## 🌈 The premise
A kid named **Leo** would rather play than do his math. When he steps into the
game, he becomes the **Tiger**, and **Stackadoo World** comes alive — the
animals talk, the mythical creatures (unicorn, dragon, fairy, wizard) roam, and
the robots help out. To get anywhere — drive the fire truck, cross the bridge,
win the card game — somebody has to solve a little math or fix a sentence.
Leo gets stuck, gets frustrated, and *gets there*. That's the whole show.

**Real-world frame (this is literally your family):** Leo plays; **Mom** watches
over his shoulder so he doesn't peek at the answers. Cut into the game world for
the adventure, cut back for the win. Authentic > polished.

## ⭐ Main cast (the "real" characters)

### 🐯 Leo — the Tiger  *(avatar: `tiger`)*
- **Who:** The kid. Restless, wants to play the card game, NOT do worksheets.
- **Flaw:** Gets frustrated fast, wants to give up (or cheat).
- **Arc every short:** stuck → grumbles → tries → small win → secretly proud.
- He's the audience stand-in. Kids see themselves in Leo.
- *(Swap option: `kinglion` 🦁 if you'd rather Leo be the Lion.)*

### 🦊 Fiona — the Queen Fox  *(avatar: `queenfox`)*
- **Who:** The striver. Fast, clever, a little bit of a show-off.
- **Flaw:** Knows-it-all energy; sometimes needs to learn patience / kindness.
- **Role:** Leo's friendly rival. She races ahead, then circles back to help —
  her growth is learning that helping > winning.

### 🐼 Mom — the Panda  *(avatar: `panda`)*
- **Who:** You. Warm, watchful, calm. The grown-up who keeps Leo honest.
- **Running gag:** "Leo… no peeking 👀." Patient, never mean. The safety blanket
  of the whole brand — signals *kind, safe, parent-made*.
- *(Swap option: `deer` 🦌 if you want a sportier mom.)*

## 📜 Canon (the rules of the world)
- **The Stackadoo Coin.** Each kid gets a glowing coin. Hold it to your heart,
  say **"Stackadoo!"**, and you're whisked into the world for help. This is the
  brand catchphrase — kids will say it out loud. 🪙
- **The moral engine.** Learning is *power and protection* — a kid who can do
  the math can't be cheated, tricked, or shortchanged. Every episode proves it.
- **Avatar hook (future):** the coin can let a kid take an animal form — Leo →
  Tiger, Fiona → Queen Fox — tying the cartoons to the playable avatars.

## 😈 The nemesis

### 🐉 Murk — the Fog Dragon  *(avatar: `dragon`, dark/stormy recolor)*
- **Who:** A gloomy dragon who breathes **"muddle fog"** (gray brain-fog)
  instead of fire. Where his fog drifts, kids' heads go fuzzy — they can't count,
  can't read, can't think.
- **Why:** Confused kids are *easy to cheat.* **Murk is a shape-shifter** — he
  doesn't just *help* swindlers, he disguises himself AS them: the shortchanging
  ice-cream man, the rigged-scale grocer, the carnival barker, the "half the
  pizza?" trickster. Different scam every episode, same dragon underneath. He
  profits from ignorance.
- **His tell (let kids spot it):** in any disguise, his eyes glint **gray-green**
  and a curl of fog leaks from his sleeve. When the kids unmask him with the
  right skill, the disguise melts into fog and he flees as the dragon, vowing
  to return. **This is the franchise's format engine** — a new Murk con each week.
- **His weapon is a feeling:** the fog whispers *"It's too hard… just give up…"*
  — he's the voice of *I can't*, which makes him Leo's true mirror, and beatable
  the same way: by trying, learning, and the coin (which burns the fog away).
- **Every episode:** Murk fogs a kid → the kid learns the skill with a teacher →
  the fog clears, the cheat is exposed, Murk fumes and slinks off to scheme again.
- *(In-game later: a "Shadow Dragon" skin, or a boss whose riddles you beat with math.)*

## 🔁 The repeatable format — *"Stuck? Step into Stackadoo World"*
A SERIES, not random clips. ~30 seconds, same shape every time so it becomes a
recognizable brand:

1. **(0–5s) The wall.** Leo wants to do the fun thing (drive, card game, slide)
   but a problem blocks him. He groans: *"Ugh, I can't!"*
2. **(5–20s) The helper.** A Stackadoo World character shows the trick — one
   clear, kid-sized concept (skip-counting, carrying a ten, "an" before a vowel).
3. **(20–28s) The win.** Leo solves it, the fun thing happens, confetti.
4. **(28–30s) Tag.** *"Play free at Stackadoo.com 🧱"* + the bear-block logo.

**One concept per short.** Don't teach two things. Volume + consistency wins on
Shorts, so a tight template you can refill is the goal.

## 🧪 Sample beat — "Leo and the Drawbridge" (subtraction)
- Leo (Tiger) races the fire truck toward a drawbridge. It's UP. A sign: "Lower
  me: 12 − 5." Leo: *"Noooo, I just wanna drive!"*
- Bolt the Robot rolls up: *"Twelve fingers… take away five… count back: 11, 10,
  9, 8, 7!"* Leo counts on his claws.
- Leo yells *"SEVEN!"*, the bridge slams down, he ROARS across in the fire truck.
- Tag: *"Get unstuck. Play free at Stackadoo.com."*

## 🪜 How it loops back into the game (the flywheel)
1. Kid sees the cartoon → loves the Tiger / Queen Fox / Bolt.
2. Recognizes them as **playable avatars** in the game.
3. Later: they appear as **teacher NPCs** (Wiz at the math meadow, Spark at the
   spelling bee, Bolt for patterns) — so the brand the cartoons build pays off
   *inside* the product, and parents trust a name they already know.

## 📐 Production notes
- **Channels:** one clip → YouTube Shorts + TikTok + IG Reels + FB Reels.
- **YouTube reality:** "Made for Kids" disables ads/comments/growth tools, so
  treat YouTube as *awareness*, measure by **clicks to the site**, and lean on
  TikTok for reach.
- **Cheap path:** SuperGrok for generation is fine — voxel-cartoony looks good.
- **Keep the cast ORIGINAL** (no Steve/Creepers) so the brand is ownable.
- **Validate lean:** batch 5–10 shorts, run a month, double down on what hits.
