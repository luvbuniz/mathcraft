# 🍦 Leo and the Ice Cream Man — Grok 30s Extend Chain

Full ~2-minute story as **four ~30-second clips**. Generate Clip 1, grab its
**last frame**, feed that frame + Clip 2's prompt as an *extend*, repeat. Each
clip re-locks the character looks (cheap insurance for consistency) and ends on a

**STYLE (already carried by the extend frame, but keep it on Clip 1):**
> Bright cartoony **voxel-cube** animation — soft rounded blocks, sunny and warm,
> smooth toy-like motion, cheerful music, clear friendly kids' voices. 16:9, 720p,
> no on-screen text. Original cube characters (NOT Minecraft skins).

**CHARACTER LOCKS (paste on every clip):**
> LEO — boy, messy brown hair, yellow t-shirt, blue shorts. FIONA — girl, brown
> hair, purple dress. SPARK — glowing lavender fairy, sparkly wings, a wand.
> ICE CREAM MAN — towering, greasy-grinned man, cold stare. Keep all looks identical
> across clips.

## CLIP 1 (0–30s) — The five dollars & the cheat
*(Start fresh. Include the STYLE + CHARACTER LOCKS above.)*

> A sunny blocky neighborhood porch. MOM smiles warmly and hands a crisp **$5 bill**
> to LEO and FIONA: "One cone each — bring me the change!" The kids cheer and race
> down the sidewalk to a jingling **ice cream truck** (sign: "Cones $3.50"). The
> towering, greasy-grinned ICE CREAM MAN slaps down two cones, takes the $5, makes a
> big show of counting, then drops just **two tiny coins (50¢)** into Leo's palm and
> **SLAMS the window, cackling**. Leo and Fiona stare at the two little coins,
> confused.
>
> **ENDS ON:** Leo and Fiona at the truck window, looking down at two small coins in
> Leo's open palm, puzzled.

## CLIP 2 (30–60s) — The fall & Stackadoo World
*(Extend from Clip 1's last frame. Re-paste CHARACTER LOCKS.)*

> Continue: Leo and Fiona stare at the two coins, heads swimming, unsure if it's
> right. Fiona turns the coins over, baffled. Leo's cheeks burn; embarrassed and
> frustrated, he bolts away — and the sidewalk **opens into a swirling glowing
> portal**. He tumbles in with a yelp; Fiona gasps "LEO!" and leaps after him. They
> fall and land softly in a dazzling **rainbow voxel wonderland** — candy-bright
> blocks, floating islands. A gentle sparkle: **SPARK the fairy** flutters down, warm
> and kind, leaning toward the muddled kids.
>
> **ENDS ON:** Spark the fairy hovering close to Leo and Fiona in the bright voxel
> world, about to speak.

## CLIP 3 (60–90s) — The lesson & the coin
*(Extend from Clip 2's last frame. Re-paste CHARACTER LOCKS.)*

> Continue: SPARK waves her wand and glowing **coins float up over a number path**.
> She counts UP with them: "Your cones were **$3.50** — you paid **$5**. Start at
> $3.50… add **fifty cents**, that's **$4.00**… add **a dollar**, that's **$5.00**!
> So your change is **one dollar fifty!**" The muddle in Leo's head clears into
> sparkles; his eyes go bright: "But he only gave me FIFTY CENTS — he kept a whole
> dollar!" Spark smiles and presses a **glowing gold Stackadoo coin** (bear-block
> logo) into each palm. The coins flash and a portal gently sweeps the kids up.
>
> **ENDS ON:** Leo and Fiona mid-swirl in the portal, glowing gold coins in hand,
> determined smiles.

## CLIP 4 (90–120s) — The catch & sweet ending
*(Extend from Clip 3's last frame. Re-paste CHARACTER LOCKS.)*

> Continue: Leo and Fiona step back out at the **ice cream truck**. A confident Leo
> holds up the coins to the Ice Cream Man as a crowd of kids gathers: "Two cones are
> **$3.50**. I paid **$5**. My change is **$1.50** — you only gave me fifty cents.
> You owe me **a whole dollar more!**" Other kids shout "He shorted me too!" A
> **SHERIFF** strolls up, badge gleaming, and hauls the sputtering Ice Cream Man away
> as the kids cheer. A cheerful **new owner** opens the truck and hands every kid a
> tall double cone, counting the change correctly. Leo and Fiona clink their glowing
> coins and grin. Fade to a clean bear-block **STACKADOO** logo (logo only).
>
> **ENDS ON:** The STACKADOO bear-block logo, centered, on a soft sky-blue background.

## Tips
- **Re-lock looks every clip** — extends drift after a few hops; restating Leo/Fiona/
  Spark/villain keeps them on-model.
- **Spoken math only** — never put numbers as on-screen text (keeps it
  language-agnostic + clean).
- If a hand-off frame looks off, regenerate just that clip; you only need its *last
  frame* to feed the next.
- Final file → **`icecream.mp4`** → send it over and it drops straight into Grammar
  Cinema (the `icecream` slot is already wired).
